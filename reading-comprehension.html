Текст 2: The Benefits of Regular Exercise

Regular physical exercise offers a multitude of benefits that extend beyond simple weight management. Engaging in activities like brisk walking, jogging, swimming, or cycling for at least 150 minutes per week can significantly improve cardiovascular health, strengthening the heart and improving blood circulation. Exercise also plays a vital role in mental well-being. It's known to reduce stress, anxiety, and symptoms of depression, partly due to the release of endorphins, which are natural mood boosters. Furthermore, consistent physical activity can improve sleep quality, increase energy levels throughout the day, and even enhance cognitive functions such as memory and concentration. It’s never too late to start incorporating more movement into your daily routine.

Текст 2: The Gig Economy

The gig economy, characterized by short-term contracts or freelance work as opposed to permanent jobs, has seen a dramatic surge in recent years. Driven by digital platforms that connect service providers with customers, it offers flexibility for workers and often lower costs for consumers. Proponents highlight the autonomy and entrepreneurial opportunities it provides. However, critics point to a lack of job security, inconsistent income, and the absence of benefits like health insurance or paid leave, which are typically associated with traditional employment. The debate continues on how to regulate this evolving labor market to ensure fair conditions for gig workers while preserving the innovation and convenience it brings.

Уровень C1 (Advanced)

Текст 1: The Subjectivity of Historical Narratives

The interpretation of historical events is seldom a monolithic endeavor; rather, it is a complex tapestry woven from diverse perspectives, biases, and the inherent limitations of available evidence. Historians, despite striving for objectivity, are inevitably influenced by their own cultural, social, and temporal contexts. Consequently, historical narratives are not static truths etched in stone but are continuously re-evaluated and reinterpreted as new evidence emerges or as societal values shift. The very act of selecting which events to highlight and which to omit can profoundly shape the resultant narrative. This inherent subjectivity underscores the importance of engaging with multiple historical accounts and critically analyzing the methodologies and motivations of historians. It is a reminder that history is as much about the present's dialogue with the past as it is about the past itself.

Текст 2: Cognitive Biases in Decision Making

Human decision-making is rarely a purely rational process. Cognitive biases, systematic patterns of deviation from norm or rationality in judgment, often unconsciously shape our perceptions and choices. For instance, confirmation bias leads individuals to favor information that confirms their preexisting beliefs, while discounting contradictory evidence. Anchoring bias occurs when people rely too heavily on an initial piece of information (the "anchor") when making subsequent judgments. The availability heuristic makes us overestimate the likelihood of events that are easily recalled in memory, often because they are recent or vivid. Recognizing these and other biases is the first step towards mitigating their influence and fostering more objective, well-reasoned decision-making, both in personal life and professional contexts.
